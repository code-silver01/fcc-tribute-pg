Tribute to Pre-Course Me

Here Lies the Brave Soul Who Thought Web Dev Was Just HTML

Ohhhh.....You too? Welcome to the family!

“I just wanted to center a div...” — Pre-Course Me, right before the meltdown.

Once upon a Git-less time, there existed a naive soul who believed websites were built by dragging buttons into place. And that <div> meant “divide and conquer.”

Their first project? A blinking, Comic Sans-fueled monstrosity. Their second project? Still blinking.

Then came CSS. They discovered margin: auto; and used it like a hammer on every nail — yet nothing centered. Flexbox sounded like a protein supplement. Grid? Witchcraft.

JavaScript brought a new flavor of pain. They asked, “What’s NaN?” The console replied: “Welcome to debugging.”

But through the chaos, they evolved. From console.log() abuse to proper semicolon placement, they emerged... not perfect, but functional. Like a site that only breaks in IE11.
